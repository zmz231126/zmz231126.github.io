1.2 EIGHT GREAT IDEAS

Created2024-05-17|Updated2024-05-17
|Post Views:

design for Moore’s law

The one constant for computer designers is rapid change.
Moore’s law indicates that the integrated circuit resources double every 18-24 months.
The computer architects should anticipate where the technology will be when the design finishes rather than where it starts.

use abstraction to simplify design

Both architects and programmers had to invent techniques to make themselves more productive, for otherwise, desigh time would lengthen as dramatically as resources grew by Moore’s law. Using abstraction, lower-level details are hidden to offer a simpler model at high levels.

make the common case fast

Making the common case fast will tend to enhance performance better than optimizing the rare case.

performance via parallelism

Since the dawn of computing, computer architects have offered designs that get more performance by computing operations in parallel.

performance via pipelining

The townfolks form a human chain to carry a water source to fire, as they could much more quickly move buckets up the chain instead of individuals running back and forth.

performance via prediction

In some cases, it can be faster on average to guess and start working rather than wait untill you know for sure.

hierarchy of memories

Programmers want the memory to be fast, large and cheap, as memory often shapes performance, capacity limits the size of problems that can be sovled. Architects find they can address these conflicting demands with a hierachy of memories, with the fastes, smallest, and the most expensive per bit at the top, and the slowest, largest, and the cheapest per bit at the bottom.

dependability via redundancy

Since any physical device can fail, we make systems dependable by including redundant components that can take over when a failure occurs and to help detect failures.
